      EXPERTISE AND RESEARCH INTERESTS

      Metabolomics, chemical proteomics, metabolite-protein interactions
      My research focuses on developing and implementing an array of novel analytical tools in the area of metabolomics and chemical proteomics to elucidate the direct binding targets of small molecules including natural medicines and endogenous metabolites, and gain new insights into how these functional molecules regulate protein function and signaling network. The gained knowledge is thus utilized to design and synthesize novel ligands that allow modulating the structures and hence the activities of the target proteins that are intimately associated with diseases such as cancer and immune diseases.

    Ning Wan,Ph.D.
    graduate student

    Education experience,
    2017-current: Ph.D in Pharmacokinetics, China Pharmaceutical University
    2013-2017: B.S. in Pharmacy, China Pharmaceutical University

    Research Field,
    Ning Wan’s main research interests are the establishment of new proteomics techniques to identify the binding targets for ligands in complex cell milieu. Collaborative efforts have given birth to a novel approach named “Target Responsive Accessibility Profiling (TRAP)” enabling the mapping of a global targetome for glycolytic metabolites exemplified by lactate in native cancer cells. Recently, her interests were also drawn into the discovery of a new PTM derived from lactate, lactylation, and hence she was striving to discover the lactylation proteome expanding beyond histones.

    Langlang Lv
    graduate student

    Research interest:Langlang is interested in mapping unknown redox post-translational modification and elucidating the biological consequences. He is now exploring S-nitrosylation, a cysteine redox post-translational modification that plays an important role in cellular signaling and impacts protein function and stability. We have designed a clickable chemical probe to label SNO-carriers in cell lysates and cells with the flexibility to exploit this probe into a wide range of cellular and biochemical assays. We are expecting this can replace the classic biotin switch technique (BST) for capturing this modification.
